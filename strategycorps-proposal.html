StrategyCorps Positioning Workshop

Strategic Positioning & Go-to-Market Clarity | Prepared by BrandThnk

Objective

Bring the leadership team together to clarify how StrategyCorps should talk about the business today, where it is headed, and how to communicate that in a way that is clear, concise, and easy for bankers to understand.

Why now

StrategyCorps has a strong existing business, a bigger growth story taking shape, and an opportunity to sharpen how those pieces come together in market. The challenge is less about vision and more about clarity: how to describe the company, the offering set, and the value in a way that helps leadership, sales, and marketing all tell the same story.

Approach

I'd recommend a two-day working session with the leadership team focused on three questions:

  • How should StrategyCorps describe what it is today?
  • How should Bezing and MonetizeIQ fit together in the story?
  • What is the clearest, most banker-friendly way to talk about the value going forward?

The goal would be to leave with a shared direction, clearer language, and a practical foundation the team can use in board conversations, sales discussions, and future marketing work.

What this work will produce

  • A clearer company story
  • A clearer way to talk about Bezing and MonetizeIQ together
  • Draft positioning language and message themes
  • Priority problems, outcomes, and use cases to lead with
  • Guidance for how the story should show up in sales and marketing

Timing

Given the desire to have a framework in place before the July board meeting, I'd suggest holding the workshop in June with a short synthesis afterward.

Format

  • Light pre-work
  • Two-day leadership workshop
  • Summary and recommendations afterward

Optional follow-on support

If useful, I can also help translate the work into materials such as messaging, sales storylines, web copy, or product marketing support.

Proposed Workshop Agenda

Day 1

Focus: get clear on the story

  • Where the business is today
  • Where the business is going
  • How leadership wants to describe that evolution
  • How Bezing and MonetizeIQ should fit together
  • What bankers need to hear — and what language is likely to confuse them
  • Initial directions for the positioning

Output by end of day:

A shared point of view on the company story and the main positioning paths worth pursuing.

Day 2

Focus: turn that story into usable language

  • Review and refine the strongest positioning direction
  • Decide what should lead the story: platform, problem, outcome, or use case
  • Define the clearest banker-friendly language
  • Identify the strongest proof points and examples
  • Outline how the story should carry into sales conversations and supporting materials
  • Align on next steps

Output by end of day:

A clear positioning direction, working message framework, and practical guidance for how the team should start using it.

Timing

Given the goal of having a framework in place ahead of the July board meeting, I'd suggest holding the workshop in June and wrapping synthesis shortly afterward.
