Executive Summary

Digital Onboarding's LinkedIn presence is underperforming relative to its market position, competitive set, and business objectives. The company has 7K followers, posts 2-3 times per week, and generates minimal engagement on the vast majority of its content.

The core problem is not frequency. It's content type, voice, and strategic intent.

Over the last 30 days, the median post earned 0-2 reactions and 0 comments. The one outlier (44 reactions, 4 comments) was a founder origin story comparing Digital Onboarding's journey to Steve Jobs' return to Apple. That post worked because it had a human story, a point of view, and emotional texture. Everything else on the page reads like product marketing copy posted to a social channel.

Meanwhile, your competitive intelligence dashboard confirms the urgency. Digital Onboarding's organic web traffic is down 29.3% year-over-year with zero paid search spend. nCino runs 1,013 active LinkedIn ads. Narmi leads with named-client proof-point ads showing specific metrics ($22M in deposits in 120 days, 89% digital adoption in 90 days). Fenergo pairs gated research reports with retargeting display campaigns. Digital Onboarding runs 15 ads total, all static images.

This audit covers 10 posts published within the review window. It assesses topic relevance, content structure, visual strategy, CTAs, posting patterns, and competitive positioning. The report concludes with a Start/Stop/Continue framework for immediate action.

Post-by-Post Audit

Each post is assessed across five dimensions: topic relevance, structure and voice, visual strategy, CTA effectiveness, and engagement outcome. Posts are ordered chronologically from most recent.

Post 1: "It takes just 5 minutes to lose a customer"

Published: ~March 11, 2026 (5 days ago)
Media: Static image
Engagement: 0 reactions, 2 comments
CTA: Webinar registration (March 18 with Jen Packard)

Topic relevance:

Strong. Post-account-opening engagement is DO's core territory. The opening hook (5 minutes to lose a customer) is specific and attention-grabbing.

Structure and voice:

Weak. The post is over 200 words and reads like a blog post dropped into LinkedIn. It cycles through rhetorical questions ("Are you guiding them to set up direct deposit or sending generic emails?") without landing a single concrete data point or named example. The voice is generic "thought leadership" rather than authoritative fintech. Multiple one-line paragraphs create a fragmented, AI-generated feel.

Visual:

Static image (appears to be a branded graphic). No native video, no carousel, no data visualization. LinkedIn's algorithm deprioritizes static images with text overlays compared to native video and document carousels.

CTA:

Buried at the bottom after 200+ words. By the time a reader gets there, they've already scrolled past. The webinar topic is strong but the CTA doesn't create urgency or state the specific takeaway.

Verdict: Right topic, wrong execution. This should be a 3-4 sentence post with one hard stat, one specific example, and a clear CTA above the fold.

Post 2: Centric Credit Union Customer Story

Published: ~March 9, 2026 (1 week ago)
Media: Static image
Engagement: 2 reactions, 0 comments
CTA: None (implicit "look at our customer")

Topic relevance:

High. Customer stories are the single most valuable content type for B2B fintech. Kelli Green at Centric CU sharing a Refer-a-Friend campaign result (159 referrals) is exactly the kind of proof point DO needs.

Structure and voice:

Missed opportunity. The post buries the number (159 referrals) in the middle of a paragraph. The opening ("We loved hearing from Kelli Green") centers DO, not the customer outcome. Compare to Narmi's approach: "$22M in new deposits in 120 days" is the headline. The number IS the hook.

Visual:

Static image. Should be a quote card with Kelli's face, her title, and the 159 referrals number in large type. People engage with people.

CTA:

None. No ask, no link, no next step. This is a wasted distribution opportunity.

Verdict: Best raw material in the entire feed. Worst packaging. Restructure: lead with the number, quote the customer, add a CTA to a case study or demo.

Post 6: Steve Jobs / Founder Origin Story

Published: ~February 16, 2026 (1 month ago)
Media: None (text only)
Engagement: 44 reactions, 4 comments
CTA: Implicit (follow the journey)

This is the highest-performing post in the audit window by a wide margin.

Why it worked:

It told a real story. The post traces DO's origin from Sales Brief to its partnership with DCU to the company it is today. It names real people and real milestones. It draws a Steve Jobs parallel that creates emotional resonance without being heavy-handed. LinkedIn's algorithm rewards posts that generate dwell time and comments. Story-driven content does both.

The lesson: Human stories from named people outperform product marketing on LinkedIn by 10-20x. This is not a nice-to-have. It's the entire strategy.

Thematic Findings

1. Content Mix Is Inverted

The current mix is roughly 70% company-centric thought leadership, 20% event/conference coverage, and 10% customer stories. For a B2B fintech company page, the optimal mix inverts this: 40-50% customer proof points (named clients, specific metrics), 20-30% people-led content (team POVs, founder stories), 15-20% industry POV (with data), and 10-15% company news/events.

2. No Consistent Human Voice

Every post comes from the company page. None come from Ted, Nate, Adam, or Jen as personal posts amplified by the company. The single highest-engagement post (44 reactions) was a founder story. LinkedIn's algorithm gives personal profiles 5-10x more organic reach than company pages.

3. Visuals Are Not Competitive

Of the 10 posts reviewed, 4 used static images, 4 had no media at all, and 2 linked to articles. The feed has no native video, no document carousels, and no data visualizations. LinkedIn's algorithm currently rewards (in order): native video, document carousels/PDFs, image posts, and text-only posts.

4. CTAs Are Inconsistent and Buried

Of 10 posts, only 2 had a clear CTA (the webinar registration and the GAC conference meeting link). The remaining 8 had no ask whatsoever. Posts without CTAs are brand impressions at best. For a company with declining organic traffic and zero paid search, every post needs to drive action.

5. Posting Timing and Frequency

Frequency (2-3x/week) is adequate but not the problem. The issue is clustering: multiple posts in the same week covering the same abstract theme while weeks go by without a customer story or product proof point. There is no visible editorial calendar logic.

Competitive Context from Intelligence Dashboard

Your competitive intelligence dashboard reveals three dynamics that make this LinkedIn underperformance more urgent:

1. The traffic gap is widening.

nCino averages 69,541 monthly visits vs. DO's 4,179. That's a 17x gap. nCino's organic traffic grew 122.9% while DO's declined 29.3%. LinkedIn is one of the few channels where DO can close this gap without massive paid spend.

2. Competitors are investing heavily in LinkedIn.

nCino runs 1,013 active LinkedIn ads across video, thought leadership, and static formats. Fenergo runs 176 ads featuring gated research reports and named thought leaders. Even Narmi (comparable market position to DO) runs 101 ads with named-client case studies. DO runs 15.

3. DO's referral network is declining.

Referral traffic is down 20.4% with no new high-impact sources identified. LinkedIn content that gets shared by partners, customers, and industry peers is one of the primary ways to rebuild referral traffic organically.

Start / Stop / Continue

Prioritized actions based on the audit findings and competitive intelligence.

START

These are new actions to introduce immediately.

# Action Why It Matters
S1 Launch personal LinkedIn content from Ted, Nate, and Adam (1 post/week each, amplified by company page) Personal profiles get 5-10x more reach than company pages. The Steve Jobs post proved this. People productization starts here.
S2 Create a named-client proof-point series: one customer story per week with a specific metric in the headline Narmi's entire ad strategy is built on this. DO has the stories (Centric CU, 25-60% attrition data) but isn't packaging them.
S3 Produce 1-2 native videos per month (60-90 seconds): customer testimonials, product demos, or POV clips from Ted/Nate Zero video in current feed. LinkedIn algorithm heavily favors native video. nCino and Fenergo both use video ads.
S4 Add a CTA to every single post (demo link, case study, webinar, report download) 80% of current posts have no CTA. With declining organic traffic and zero paid search, every organic post must drive action.
S5 Gate a benchmark report ("Community Bank Onboarding Benchmarks 2026") and promote it as a document carousel on LinkedIn Fenergo's playbook: gated reports + retargeting display ads drove +197.5% display growth. DO should replicate this for engagement data.
S6 Expand the 2x2x2 attack messaging from ads into organic content (posts, carousels, short videos) The competitive intelligence dashboard flagged this as DO's strongest ad hook. It's not showing up in organic content at all.

STOP

These patterns are actively hurting engagement and credibility.

# Action Why
X1 Stop posting abstract thought leadership with no data, no named examples, and no CTA Posts 3, 4, 8, and 9 all follow this pattern. Zero engagement on all of them. This is the #1 content type on the page and the lowest performer.
X2 Stop posting text-only content without media 4 of 10 posts had no visual at all. Text-only posts earn the lowest algorithmic reach on LinkedIn unless the author has a massive personal following.
X3 Stop vague competitive positioning posts that hint at competitors without naming them or providing evidence The "imitation" post reads as insecure. If DO wants to claim market leadership, show it with data and customer outcomes.
X4 Stop off-topic newsjacking (Black Friday trends, generic AI commentary) that doesn't connect to DO's core value proposition These posts dilute positioning. Every post should reinforce: DO = post-account-opening engagement for community banks and credit unions.

CONTINUE

These are working. Do more of them.

# Action Why
C1 Founder and origin stories (the Steve Jobs/DCU post) 44 reactions and 4 comments vs. near-zero on everything else. This format works. Do it monthly with different team members.
C2 Customer stories with named contacts (Kelli Green at Centric CU) The raw material is strong. Fix the packaging (lead with the metric, quote the person, add a CTA) and this becomes the backbone of the content strategy.
C3 Conference presence posts with meeting CTAs The GAC post had the clearest CTA on the page. Keep doing event coverage but pair it with a hard proof point.
C4 Webinar promotion posts (Jen Packard sessions) Webinars convert. The format needs refinement (shorter posts, CTA above the fold) but the content type is right.

Bottom Line

The proof points exist (25-60% attrition reduction, 159 referrals at Centric CU, 170+ FI customers). The people exist (Ted, Nate, Jen, Adam). The competitive positioning exists (the 2x2x2 attack angle, the post-origination gap).

What's missing is the discipline to put those assets into formats that LinkedIn rewards: short posts with hard numbers, native video, document carousels, personal-profile content from named leaders, and a CTA on every single post.

The competitive intelligence dashboard makes the cost of inaction clear. Organic traffic is declining. Competitors are spending. The referral network is weakening. LinkedIn is one of the few channels where DO can build visibility without a six-figure ad budget, but only if the content strategy changes.

Recommended next step: Build a 4-week editorial calendar using the Start/Stop/Continue framework above. Prioritize S1 (personal content from Ted and Nate) and S2 (named-client proof-point series) as the first two moves.
