Executive Summary

The Validation Sprint has reached its synthesis phase. Seven stakeholder interviews, combined with pre-engagement research and market analysis, confirm three core findings:

  1. Horicon Bank has strong foundations but needs persona consolidation to make them actionable. Current segments (~13 across life stages, retail, and business sectors) describe demographics. Validated personas should describe aspirations. This reframe makes them usable by frontline teams.
  2. "Together. Doing Good." is the brand position with universal internal support and market-ready differentiation. Every stakeholder independently confirmed this positioning over "The Natural Choice." It works across retail and business, connects to operational proof points (volunteer time, trust seminars, nonprofit partnerships), and aligns with how Milwaukee and Gen Z make banking decisions.
  3. The bank's growth bottleneck is not capability or budget but strategic focus and frontline activation. The tech stack is solid. The programs exist. The challenge is saying no to some things, simplifying the persona architecture, and getting this strategy into the hands of branch managers and BDOs who talk to customers every day.

This document synthesizes interview findings into validated conclusions and sets up Week 4 Recommendations Brief.

Persona Consolidation: From Segments to Aspirations

The Shift

Current state: ~13 segments across 5 life stages (M2-M4, Y1, F1), 3 retail personas (School Daze, Fiscal Rookies, Upwardly Mobile), and 5 business sectors (Professional Services, Manufacturing, Real Estate, Agriculture, Nonprofits).

Validated target: 3-4 aspiration-first personas. This consolidation works because:

  • Fewer personas = simpler for frontline to remember and use
  • Aspiration-first framing = more intuitive than demographic codes
  • Personality-driven messaging = resonates across age, income, and life stage
  • Case study precedent: FMHC consolidated 100+ segments to 4 personas using this approach

Aspiration-First Framing: Why It Works

The shift from demographic segmentation to aspiration-first personas makes personas usable by frontline teams:

Old Approach (Demographic)

Segment: "M2 Wealthy Achiever, age 55-74, household income $150K+"

Problem: Frontline doesn't think in demographic codes. Kerry doesn't remember the M2 label.

New Approach (Aspiration)

Persona: "Established Steward. Someone protecting what they've built."

Benefit: Kerry immediately recognizes her best customers and knows what matters to them.

Organizing Principle

The aspiration becomes the organizing principle for messaging, journeys, and product positioning.

Instead of building separate campaigns for each demographic slice, all messaging for Established Stewards rolls up to "protect and transfer what you've built."

Precedent

FMHC consolidated 100+ segments to 4 personas using this approach. Same result: simpler for frontline, more powerful for marketing, easier to measure.

Brand Position Synthesis: "Together. Doing Good."

All seven stakeholders independently confirmed "Together. Doing Good." as the stronger brand position. Here's the synthesis:

Works Across Retail and Business

Erik confirmed it in business development context. Not just a consumer brand tagline — it works for how Horicon thinks about commercial partnerships too.

Connects to Operational Proof Points

40 hours paid volunteer time for all employees. CHRE virtues. Trust seminars focused on community stewardship. Nonprofit checking program. The tagline isn't marketing fluff — it describes actual culture.

Geographic Advantage

"The Natural Choice" + Horicon Marsh reference has zero meaning in Milwaukee. "Together. Doing Good." is universally understood and resonates.

Gen Z Values Alignment

52% of Gen Z choose banks on values. "Together. Doing Good." speaks directly to this decision framework and differentiates from national banks chasing the same cohort.

Executive Buy-In

Fred built conference presentation around it. Steve had WTMJ radio "aha moment." This isn't just marketing consensus — it's leadership commitment.

Forward-Facing Positioning

130th anniversary positions longevity. "Together. Doing Good." positions purpose. Together = nostalgia + aspiration.

Recommendation: Retire "The Natural Choice" as primary brand position. Keep only in legacy Dodge County marketing where Horicon Marsh has local resonance. All growth-market messaging (Milwaukee, Fox Valley) should lead with "Together. Doing Good."

Strategic Tensions to Resolve in Working Sessions

These tensions are normal for a community bank embracing persona-led growth. They need to be acknowledged and resolved in Week 4 working sessions:

1. Breadth vs. Focus

Community bank DNA says serve everyone. Persona strategy says focus. Resolution: Personas don't exclude anyone. They prioritize investment and messaging. A solo mom in Fox Valley still gets a mortgage. But the bank won't build separate mortgage products for every demographic slice.

2. Legacy vs. Growth Markets

Established markets (Dodge County) have proven playbooks. Milwaukee is a new market that needs prospect acquisition, not just customer retention. Same personas, different activation strategies. Resolution: One brand, two go-to-market playbooks.

3. Measurement Gap

Strong metrics where Total Expert is deployed (Glider journey = 0% failure rate). No baseline for most marketing activities. Resolution: 90-day plan needs measurable KPIs from day one. Can't optimize what you don't measure.

4. Simplification vs. Ambition

Cassie: "Keep it simple, stupid." The bank has too many priorities ("squirrel problem"). Fewer personas = fewer campaigns = deeper impact. Resolution: This requires saying no to some things. Clear prioritization in 90-day plan.
