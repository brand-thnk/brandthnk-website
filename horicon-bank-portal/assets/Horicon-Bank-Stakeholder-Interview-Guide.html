Stakeholder Interview Guide

Using the Persona Explorer to Drive Discovery

Purpose

Use the persona artifact as a structured discussion tool to surface frontline reality, identify blind spots, and validate (or challenge) current assumptions.

❌ Don't

Send the artifact ahead of time and ask for feedback.

✓ Do

Walk through it live during the interview and watch where people react, disagree, or go quiet.

Interview Structure (30 minutes)

1. Setup (2 minutes)

"I've pulled together a first pass at our customer personas based on the data and programs we have. I want to walk through a couple with you and get your reaction — where this matches what you see, where it doesn't, and what's missing."

Why this works: Frames it as a draft, not a finished product. Invites disagreement. Positions them as the expert.

2. Persona Walkthrough (15 minutes)

Select 2-3 personas most relevant to their lens.

1.

Show Goals & Frustrations tab first

2.

Ask: "Does this feel right? What would you add or change?"

3.

Then show Journey tab

4.

Ask: "Where does this break down in your experience?"

Watch for:

  • • Immediate disagreement ("That's not how they think about it")
  • • Pauses or hesitation ("Well, maybe, but...")
  • • Specifics ("I had a customer last week who...")
  • • Energy shifts (where they lean in vs check out)

3. Directed Questions (10 minutes)

Pull from the "What We Need" section of relevant personas. Ask 3-4 of the most important questions for their role.

4. Close (3 minutes)

"If you could only fix one thing to reach more of [persona], what would it be?"

Why this works: Forces prioritization. Surfaces what they think actually matters vs nice-to-have.

Synthesis Questions

As you go through interviews, track:

Consensus

What do multiple people say independently?

Contradiction

Where do people disagree with each other?

Silence

What topics generate no reaction or energy?

Energy

Where do people lean in and get specific?

Why This Approach Works

✓

You're not asking for validation. You're using the personas as a provocation to surface what people actually know.

✓

You're giving them structure. The artifact shows you've done the work. Now you're refining it with their expertise.

✓

You're creating ownership. When they see their input reflected in the final version, they're invested in using it.

✓

You're building conviction. By the end, you'll know which personas are real (grounded in data + experience) vs which are aspirational (we wish this were true).

What Success Looks Like

After these interviews, you should be able to:

1.

Confidently retire or deprioritize 2-3 personas

2.

Strengthen 2-3 personas with real stories and language

3.

Identify 1 persona ready for 90-day activation

4.

Know what data/research is missing to validate the rest

5.

Have internal alignment on what's real vs aspirational

This becomes the foundation for your recommendations brief.
