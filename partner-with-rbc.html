How We Partner
With You

Most large financial institutions operate with brand in one lane and product marketing in another.

The brand side lives as a sponsorship layer that's broad and credibility-building, but hard to tie to growth. The product side runs performance campaigns that drive revenue but feel disconnected from the larger story. Unfortunately, the market ends up hearing a lot without hearing anything clearly.

True market influence works more like surround sound. BrandThnk collaborates with you to build the positioning, messaging architecture, and go-to-market model that connect brand and product into a single system that creates this surround sound effect.

“

Your customers should hear the brand in different ways, from different directions, at different moments.

Practice

We work with a maximum of two clients at a time, and our engagements are intentionally built so your team can carry it forward step by step. As a small shop, we don't win by keeping you dependent on our services.

Allison Netzer leads the work directly, supported by a team of four: a designer, a copywriter, a personal branding specialist, and operations.

Project Plan (Example)

01
Deep Discovery

Before we touch messaging or positioning, we get clear on what's broken. It's usually a misalignment: the product story is strong, the brand story is strong, but the customer hears neither clearly.

Inputs
  • Stakeholder interviews across marketing, product, sales, and executive leadership
  • Audit of existing brand, messaging, and go-to-market plans
  • Identify internal blockers and external whitespace
  • Define what breakthrough actually looks like for the business
OutputStrategy Brief with distilled insights, aligned goals, and a shared lens the rest of the engagement builds on.
02
Positioning Lab

This is where the brand and product stories become one story. For institutions with deep market credibility, the opportunity is to find the sharpest articulation of your "why now" and build messaging that flows from brand down through product lines in a coherent hierarchy.

Inputs
  • Core narrative, proof points, and audience resonance mapping
  • Clarify what you are and what you are not
  • Build a messaging hierarchy from institutional brand down to product-level campaigns
OutputNew Brand Positioning Map and Messaging Framework.
03
Go-to-Market Model

This week we build the go-to-market model grounded in business reality, where the proposed shift becomes operational: aligning product moments with brand moments so the market experiences one institution in surround sound instead of what feels like different banks.

Inputs
  • Select best-fit channels for your audiences and competitive dynamics
  • Align product moments with marketing moments
  • Define what "launch-ready" means and what follows it
Output90-Day GTM Activation Plan with milestones, roles, content hooks, and leverage points.
04
Strategy-to-Execution Bridge

This phase is about embedding the new platform into how the organization operates, and that means identifying the skeptics early and bringing them into the process. The rollout plan has to change language and behavior.

Inputs
  • Work with internal teams to prepare activation across functions
  • Set up internal documentation and operating norms
  • Coach teams and agencies on adoption and adaptation
OutputInternal Playbook.
05
Feedback Loop Setup

Markets move, and the strategy has to flex without losing its shape. This week we build the measurement rhythm that ties brand and go-to-market performance to business outcomes and gives the team a system for knowing when to adjust and when to hold.

Inputs
  • Set KPIs tied to business outcomes
  • Build a rhythm for reflection and course correction
  • Define what "working" looks like
OutputImpact Dashboard Template with guidance on how to read, report, and act on what you see.
06
Executive Strategy Session

The work only holds if leadership owns it. The final week is a working session with your senior leadership where we'll co-present the full strategy, make the case for change, and map what comes next.

Requirements

A core team of 3–5 stakeholders who can commit to weekly working sessions and have the authority to make decisions. Access to customer-facing teams and relevant data. Cross-functional participation (particularly from product and product marketing) and a willingness to hear things that might be uncomfortable.
