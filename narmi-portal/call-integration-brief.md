## By Deliverable

### 1. Narmi AI GTM Overview

**Add:** A three-wave narrative structure for the GTM arc.
- Wave 1: Internal AI tooling (table stakes, done)
- Wave 2: AI connectors and enablement — compliance-friendly, meeting FIs where they are
- Wave 3: Staff-facing agentic workflows, then eventually consumer-facing

This isn't just a product roadmap. It's a GTM sequencing story: we went in order of what FIs could actually adopt, not what made for the best press release.

**Add:** Dual-audience GTM strategy. Chris put it at 10/90 — about 10% of the ICP are self-described progressives who need to see innovation and first-to-market proof. The 90% need to see concrete 6-month impact. These are different activation strategies, not just different messaging.

For the 10%: The buzz isn't from a manifesto. It's from doing things first. MCP connector marketplace. Claude first. Decision Assist in production. Being first at doing real things is where Narmi's credibility lives.

For the 90%: ROI math (200 hrs/month saved), specific use case evidence (onboarding, fraud, customer service), and the staff redirect story — what Tammy does with the time she gets back.

**Add:** The Copilot license frame as the competitive anchor. Not a feature comparison vs. Alchemy or Q2. The real competitive question from an FI's budget perspective is: "We bought Copilot licenses and nobody's using them. Why would this be different?" Answer that directly.

**Flag:** The deliverable probably has "thought leadership strategy" as a section. Make sure it's grounded in doing, not saying. Narmi's thought leadership is first-to-market proof points published quickly, not white papers about where AI is going.

### 2. Narmi AI Narrative

**Add:** The Tammy story. This is the narrative hero — find it and anchor to it.

A community bank or credit union's loan review team spends 20 minutes on each manual case. They get 400 a month. That's potentially 133–200 hours going to review, not to members. AI Decision Assist changes that. And the time Tammy gets back? She calls and welcomes new members instead of policing paperwork. That's not productivity. That's the community banking value proposition coming back online.

This story does more work than any product description. Make sure it's in the narrative, near the front, told from the FI's perspective.

**Add:** The "scaling without hiring proportionally" framing. Both Chris and Nathan said the same thing: AI isn't reducing headcount at these institutions. It's allowing them to scale without going one-for-one on personnel. In a community bank where one person runs a department, that math is everything.

**Add:** The community banking risk distinction. This is counterintuitive and therefore differentiated: community banking workflows actually carry higher risk than enterprise workflows, not lower — because there's no seven-layer approval chain catching mistakes. That's exactly why getting AI right for CFIs matters more, not less. Narmi builds for that.

**Remove or defer:** Anything about end-user facing AI, consumer experience personalization, or the "digital banking control plane" concept. Chris floated it, then walked it back. It's not on the roadmap this year and will create expectations the product can't meet yet.

**Sharpen:** The "this isn't your first time seeing this pattern" angle. Online banking did it. Digital banking did it. AI is doing it again — first solutions built for Chase, then watered down for CFIs. Narmi is built the other way. This framing works precisely because it doesn't require FIs to understand AI. They've lived this story before.

### 3. Narmi AI Guide for Marketing

**Add:** The dual-audience structure should organize this entire guide. Progressive FIs vs. pragmatic majority aren't just segments — they require different content types, different proof points, and different channel activation.

For progressive FIs:
- Lead with first-mover proof points (Claude marketplace, Decision Assist launch, MCP)
- Avoid vision language and predictions — their sophisticated audiences see through it
- Content format: case briefs, launch announcements, technical explainers

For pragmatic majority:
- Lead with ROI math and specific use cases
- Avoid AI jargon and timelines — they're evaluating based on 6-month horizon
- Content format: problem/solution one-pagers, customer stories, implementation guides

Build activation content around what a credit buys: "Run a complete onboarding agent review for one credit. Launch a marketing campaign analysis for twelve." Make it concrete so FIs can translate the investment into recognizable work output.

**Flag:** The Copilot license comparison isn't just messaging — it's a channel activation targeting procurement conversations. Any content that reaches CFO or COO level should include the budget comparison frame. That's where the buy decision actually gets made.
