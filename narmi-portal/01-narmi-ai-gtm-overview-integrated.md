## Market Problem

Community banks and credit unions are under pressure to understand AI, improve efficiency, serve customers and members more intelligently, and compete against larger institutions without adding complexity to already stretched teams.

They know AI matters. They also know most AI solutions are not built for them. They need a partner that can help them understand, adopt, and apply AI in ways that make sense for their size institution.

---

## How We Got Here

Narmi approached AI in sequence — not because of caution, but because that is how community FIs can actually adopt it.

**Wave 1 — Internal:** AI tooling applied inside Narmi's own workflows. Proof of concept and operational learning before going to market.

**Wave 2 — AI connectors and enablement:** Compliance-friendly integrations that let FIs connect to AI platforms without creating new risk exposure. MCP is the flagship. The Claude connector marketplace is live. ChatGPT connector in progress. Each connector is a channel, not just a feature.

**Wave 3 — Staff-facing agentic workflows:** AI embedded directly into banking operations. AI Decision Assist is the marquee product. Onboarding, fraud, customer service are next. Consumer-facing applications will follow when FI readiness catches up.

This sequencing is the strategy. It reflects how community institutions can absorb AI — starting from the inside out, building confidence, then expanding.

## Customer Pain: What Are We Solving?

How do community FIs use AI to move faster, serve smarter, and compete without adding more complexity to already stretched teams? This pain is not one-dimensional. It shows up across strategy, operations, compliance, staff capacity, and customer/member expectations.

- **Strategic pressure:** AI has moved from a technology discussion to a CEO-level issue tied to productivity, staffing, operating model, and competitiveness.
- **Operational overload:** Teams are already stretched. AI needs to reduce work, not create more work.
- **Trust gap:** Paper approval is not the same as institutional confidence. FIs need transparency, accountability, logging, explainability, and clear governance.
- **Human-in-the-loop need:** Community FI leaders want AI to make people faster and better-informed, especially in workflows that affect customers or members.
- **Adoption uncertainty:** Leaders may be excited, skeptical, overwhelmed, and unsure where to start.
- **SMB expectation shift:** Small business customers are already experimenting with AI and increasingly expect financial tools that help them understand and act on their financial data.

One thing the market often gets wrong: community banking workflows carry higher operational risk than enterprise workflows — not lower. With fewer people in the approval chain, getting AI right matters more, not less.

## Narmi AI Opportunity

Narmi has practical wedges that can anchor the AI story now and near-term:

**AI for secure customer message response**
AI that helps financial institutions respond to secure customer messages faster, with more consistency, and without losing the trust and care required in banking communication.

**AI Search Bar in Narmi Command**
Conversational intelligence that helps staff ask natural-language questions, surface operational insights, and move directly from answer to action inside workflows like account opening, transfers, and wires.

**In-house marketing assistant**
AI that helps marketing teams move faster, create sharper work, and demonstrate applied AI in its own operating model.

**MCP server for business banking intelligence**
AI that lets business customers analyze cash flow, balances, and transactions with Claude or ChatGPT, without exposing sensitive data. First bank live in Claude connector marketplace the week of June 2 — likely the first bank in the world to achieve this.

**AI Decision Assist** *(near-term proof point)*
Agentic workflow for back-office operations. Handles manual reviews that typically consume significant staff time — freeing teams to redirect that capacity toward higher-value customer and member work. Proof points expected within 30–45 days. Do not publish specific metrics until confirmed.

**Narmi Agentic Deployment Force (NADF)** *(early stage)*
Forward-deployed Narmi engineers who build custom AI agents directly inside FI environments, on top of the Narmi platform. Addresses the "sticky note" problem: every institution has unique workflows that generic AI cannot reach. This is a new sales motion — a wedge into FIs that are locked in competitor contracts — not just a service offering.

## Dual Audience Framework

Community FIs are not a monolithic buyer. The GTM motion needs to account for two distinct audiences — each requiring a different lead message.

**Progressive FIs (~10% of ICP)**
These institutions self-identify as innovators. They want to see Narmi moving first. For this audience, the buzz is not about vision or prediction — it is about doing things first. The Claude marketplace launch. MCP. AI Decision Assist in production. Being the first bank live in an AI connector marketplace is what credibility looks like here. Not a manifesto.

**Pragmatic majority (~90% of ICP)**
These institutions are under real pressure from boards and leadership to have an AI answer — but they will not buy on vision. They need to see a 6-month ROI case. Concrete use cases. Staff time returned. Problems they recognize. For this audience, lead with the Tammy story, the back-office time savings, and the workflow specificity.

Both audiences need proof. The proof looks different.

## Competitive Frame

The comparison community FIs are already making internally is not Narmi vs. Alchemy or Narmi vs. Q2. It is Narmi vs. Microsoft Copilot licenses — the one AI spend most FIs have already made and mostly are not using.

For sales and marketing, anchor to this: Narmi AI is a better use of the AI budget they already have. It works inside banking workflows. It connects to the tools they already use. It is built for their operating reality in a way Copilot is not.

For context: industry data suggests a significant share of enterprise AI tool purchasers have not implemented a single workflow. Community FIs are experiencing the same gap. Narmi's value proposition is getting AI from purchase to production.
