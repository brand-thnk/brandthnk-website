Subject: The CPO who built the data layer behind Alkami -- and why it still wasn't enough

Hi Kirk,

You said it yourself on Episode 368: 95% of large companies with AI initiatives are getting zero results. You attributed it to MIT. And you asked the honest question your audience needed to hear.

I have the guest who can answer it.

Nate Shahan spent 14 years at Segmint, the data intelligence company he co-founded that classified billions of transactions for 147+ financial institutions -- building the Key Lifestyle Indicators that became the backbone of member intelligence for credit unions across the country. Alkami acquired Segmint for $135.5 million in 2022 specifically to solve the gap between data and engagement. Nate stayed on to run Alkami's data products division and watched firsthand what happened when FIs finally had the infrastructure. Some flew.

He joined Digital Onboarding as CPO in January to build what he describes as the missing chapter: a platform that closes the loop between data, engagement, and lifecycle -- purpose-built for the way community FIs actually operate.

His thesis for your audience: you cannot personalize what you cannot classify. Credit unions racing to deploy AI agents and automated engagement tools are building on sand if their member data is sitting in five systems that don't talk to each other. He's spent 20 years building the foundation, and he has a very specific point of view on what to fix before you sign your next AI contract.

Ted Brown, DO's CEO, was on CU 2.0 back in Episode 203. That episode told the engagement story. This would be the data story -- what makes engagement possible, and what breaks when the foundation isn't right.

Happy to send a one-pager with talking points if that's useful for your team!
