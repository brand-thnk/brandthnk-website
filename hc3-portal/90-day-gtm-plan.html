Direct Answer to Kristan's Question

"If HC3 were your company and resources were tight, what would you protect at all costs, and what would you scale back?"

Protect: Customer warm-up (top 10 only), leadership alignment, website platform launch, one customer case study, basic sales enablement. These five elements create the minimum viable launch that protects revenue and establishes credibility.

Scale back: Benchmark study (defer 6 months), multiple analyst briefings (focus on one), content volume (2-3 sub-brand pages, not six), press outreach (let momentum build organically), webinars (optional only if clear capacity and ROI).

Why this works: You get market validation and customer protection without overwhelming the team. The platform story gets established, sales gets equipped, and momentum builds naturally rather than artificially.

Resource Mitigation Strategies

  • Content efficiency: Template-based approach for consistent messaging across channels. Focus on adapting core platform story rather than creating net-new content
  • Customer development focus: Use existing customer success touchpoints instead of separate outreach
  • Partner leverage: Jack Henry co-marketing reduces HC3 creative burden
  • Analyst strategy: One deep relationship beats three surface briefings
  • Event optimization: If attending existing events, retrofit messaging rather than adding new events

Launch Frame: Platform Introduction

Community banks and credit unions hear "rebrand" as instability. They hear "platform" as capability. HC3 leads with platform.

The narrative is additive:

  • HC3 already runs the statement work. That is not changing.
  • The platform was always there. HC3 is naming it now.
  • The six sub-brands are how customers already use HC3. HC3 is making that architecture visible.

This framing protects the install base and gives sales a credible cross-sell story from day one.

90-Day Timeline (Bandwidth-Optimized)

Phase 1: Days 1 to 30. Foundation and Internal Alignment

Focus: Get the foundation right before going external. Sequential execution to avoid overwhelming the team.

Week 1-2: Internal Foundation

  • Leadership alignment session. 90 minutes with exec team. BrandThnk facilitates, minimal HC3 prep required.
  • Sales enablement package. One-page platform story + basic objection handling. HC3 creates with positioning framework from BrandThnk.
  • Website content development. HC3 develops platform pages using BrandThnk positioning framework and messaging guidelines.

Week 3-4: Customer Foundation

  • Customer warm launch (top 10 accounts only). Kristan and Tori brief strongest relationships. Target: 2-3 willing to provide quotes.
  • One customer case study development. HC3 team interviews and writes case study using BrandThnk messaging framework. HC3 coordinates customer approval.
  • Internal all-hands. 30-minute session. Platform story + Q&A. Everyone can explain HC3's evolution in 60 seconds.

Phase 2: Days 31 to 60. Focused Public Launch

Focus: High-impact, low-resource activities. Quality over quantity.

Week 5-6: Platform Launch

  • Website goes live. New platform messaging, 2-3 sub-brand pages (not all six), customer case study.
  • Soft launch. LinkedIn post from Tori using platform messaging framework. Customer quote block. No press release initially.
  • Sales team activation. One training session on new messaging. Account teams begin retention conversations.

Week 7-8: Targeted Amplification

  • Jack Henry SSO coordinated launch (if timing aligns). Leverage existing partnership momentum.
  • One analyst briefing with Aite-Novarica. Focus on quality relationship over quantity.
  • LinkedIn content cadence. Weekly from Tori, monthly from Jeremiah. Use platform messaging framework for consistent voice.

Phase 3: Days 61 to 90. Proof and Rhythm

Focus: Generate proof points while establishing sustainable operating rhythm.

Week 9-10: Customer Proof

  • Install-base retention focus. Sales team uses new platform story in renewal conversations. Track sub-brand attach opportunities.
  • Customer success integration. Customer success team incorporates platform story into regular touchpoints.

Week 11-12: Event and Content

  • Trade event appearance (if already committed). Retrofit messaging, don't add new events.
  • Additional sub-brand content as bandwidth allows. Target: 1-2 more pages, not all six.
  • 90-day readout to exec team. What worked, what didn't, next 90 days scope and resource requirements.

What We're NOT Doing (To Protect Bandwidth)

  • Benchmark study — Defer to Q1 2027
  • Webinars — Optional only if clear capacity and ROI
  • Three analyst briefings — Focus on one quality relationship
  • All six sub-brand content — Launch with 2-3, add others as capacity allows
  • Multiple press placements — Let momentum build organically

Success Metrics (Bandwidth-Appropriate)

  • Revenue protection: Install-base retention rate maintains or improves
  • Platform adoption: 80% of sales team can articulate platform story
  • Customer validation: 3-5 customers willing to provide platform quotes
  • Market perception: One positive analyst mention or customer reference
  • Internal alignment: Exec team consistently uses platform language in external settings
