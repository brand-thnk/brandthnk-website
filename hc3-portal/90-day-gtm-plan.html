HC3 90-Day GTM Activation Plan

BrandThnk Breakthrough • Week 4 Deliverable

Back to Deliverables

What This Plan Does

This plan turns the new positioning into traction. It reintroduces HC3 to the market as a communication platform through an additive story that protects credibility with existing customers.

Two goals organize the work: shift perception, and build the proof that makes the shift stick. Demand follows proof.

How the House of Brands Earns Its Keep

Buying journeys stay product-by-product. Prospects do not buy four HC3 products at once, and this plan does not pretend they will. The house of brands earns its keep in three places:

  1. Retention. When existing customers see HC3 as a strategic partner running six related capabilities, they stop shopping HC3 as a statement vendor.
  2. M&A inbound. When HC3 acquires, the four pillars give every target a clear slot. Acquired capabilities land inside Orchestrate, Secure, Simplify, or Scale and get renamed into the HC3 portfolio.
  3. M&A outbound. When HC3 is evaluated as an acquisition candidate, the architecture reads as a platform, not a product bundle. That changes the valuation conversation.

The GTM motions in this plan reflect that. Net-new sales runs product-first. Retention, partner, and M&A motions run platform-first.

Strategic Objectives

Primary goal: Move HC3 out of the "statement company" box and into the communication platform category in the minds of community banks, credit unions, and the analysts and partners who shape their buying lens.

Revenue protection: Retention and install-base sub-brand attach carry the near-term revenue. Net-new logos follow.

Third-party validation: Customer voices, partner validation, and analyst coverage do the repositioning work HC3 can't credibly do alone.

Timing constraint: The acquired company capability in Q4 needs a platform story to land in, which means this reintroduction has to work before then.

Launch Frame: Platform Introduction

Community banks and credit unions hear "rebrand" as instability. They hear "platform" as capability. HC3 leads with platform.

The narrative is additive:

  • HC3 already runs the statement work. That is not changing.
  • The platform was always there. HC3 is naming it now.
  • The six sub-brands are how customers already use HC3. HC3 is making that architecture visible.

This framing protects the install base and gives sales a credible cross-sell story from day one.

90-Day Timeline

Phase 1: Days 1 to 30. Internal Readiness and Customer Warm Launch.

  • Leadership alignment session (week 1). Separate from the all-hands. Purpose: give Tori and the exec team language for how the platform narrative translates outside sales and marketing. Brand as an operating lever, not a marketing asset. Closes the gap Tori flagged at kickoff.
  • Internal all-hands reintroduction. Every HC3 employee can explain the platform and name the six sub-brands in under 60 seconds.
  • Internal language retrain. Short workshop for customer-facing teams on how internal framing of customers shows up externally. Jeremiah to co-lead. Small ask, outsized payoff.
  • Sales enablement: one-page platform narrative, six sub-brand one-sheets, objection-handling guide, retention-conversation play by install-base segment.
  • Customer warm launch: Kristan and Tori personally brief the top 20 accounts on the platform story before anything goes public. Ask for permission to feature 3 to 5 as launch-day voices. (Some will say no. Plan for it.)
  • Analyst pre-briefings under embargo with Aite-Novarica, Celent, Javelin.
  • Partner pre-briefings with top two core providers. Include Jack Henry if the SSO partnership supports it.
  • Content pipeline built: launch blog, six sub-brand pages, one customer case study ready, two LinkedIn essays from Tori and Jeremiah drafted.
  • Benchmark study scoped. Survey instrument designed, sample target set (250 community banks and credit unions), fielding partner selected. Publication targeted for late Phase 3.
  • Launch-ready checklist signed off by Kristan, Tori, and BrandThnk.

Phase 2: Days 31 to 60. Public Launch and Demand Activation.

  • Launch day (Week 5). Platform announcement. New positioning live on site. LinkedIn post from Tori. Press release to trade press, not general business media. Customer quote block included.
  • Week 6. HC3 Verified feature story placement. Target: American Banker or BAI Banking Strategies. Pair with Jeremiah byline on fraud and orchestration. Check obfuscation is the product anchor.
  • Week 7. Jack Henry SSO coordinated launch. First Century Bank as named live client. Jeremiah white paper published. Press pickup in core banking trade press. Podcast appearances (one bank-focused, one credit union-focused).
  • Week 8. Partner co-marketing goes live with at least one core banking provider. Webinar co-hosted with a customer on vendor consolidation. Analyst report or quote from at least one of the three briefed analysts.

Demand motion in parallel:

  • Weekly LinkedIn cadence from Tori (platform narrative) and Jeremiah (contrarian, fraud, and technical angle).
  • ABM outreach to the 50 target accounts using the new platform story. Account-team led.
  • Email re-engagement to stalled pipeline with platform reframe.
  • Retention conversations with install base: sub-brand attach opportunities identified per account.

Phase 3: Days 61 to 90. Proof, Pipeline, and Post-Launch Rhythm.

  • Flagship trade event appearance. HC3 shows up as Communication Orchestration, not as a statement vendor. Booth narrative and session topic both anchored to the platform story. (Event committed in Phase 1 based on fall community bank and credit union calendars.)
  • Second customer case study published, this time tied to HC3 Campaign or HC3 Connect to prove the platform claim.
  • Benchmark study published. "State of Bank-to-Customer Communication" with a small, defensible data set. Pitched as source-material for trade press. HC3 owns a cited data point competitors do not have. (Response rate is always a question mark with these studies. 250 target, plan for 75 complete responses.)
  • Webinar two. Compliance and archive story for risk and audit audience.
  • Mid-cycle content refresh. Platform explainer video, short and product-anchored.
  • Install-base retention campaign runs against the bottom two-thirds of the customer list. Target: one new sub-brand attach per account where the opportunity exists.
  • Readout to HC3 exec team on pipeline, press, perception signals, and next-90 operating rhythm. Tied into EOS quarterly planning so GTM sits inside the VTO and rocks, not next to it.
