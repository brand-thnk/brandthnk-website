Track 1: Executive Questions (Tori VanCura-Rutland)

▼
  • Market Reality Check
    "You've been driving growth in a market where HC3 is known primarily as a 'statement company.' Can you walk me through a recent prospect conversation where you had to explain why HC3 is more than just statements? What language resonated, and where did you lose them?"
  • Competitive Battlefield
    "When you're going head-to-head with companies like Doxim or Messagepoint, what's the one thing you wish prospects understood about HC3 that they consistently miss? Give me a specific example of a deal you should have won but didn't - what was the deciding factor?"
  • Growth Ceiling
    "Looking at your current partnerships and BD pipeline, what's the biggest barrier preventing HC3 from growing faster? Is it market perception, product gaps, pricing, or something else? Where do you see the ceiling, and what would it take to break through it?"
  • AI Positioning Opportunity
    "There's a lot of talk about AI backlash creating opportunities for 'efficiency without risk' solutions. From your conversations with FI prospects, how real is this anxiety, and how could HC3 capitalize on it without sounding like we're fear-mongering?"
  • 18-Month Vision
    "Kristan mentioned wanting HC3 to be seen as the company that 'solves what the core cannot.' If that positioning works perfectly, what does your typical sales conversation look like 18 months from now? How do prospects describe HC3 to their boards?"

Track 2: Frontline Questions (Matt Engle)

▼
  • Client Reality
    "When you're managing projects for existing clients, what do they actually say HC3 does best? Not the marketing speak, but the real language they use when describing HC3 to their own teams or other FIs?"
  • Implementation Pain Points
    "What's the most common thing that goes wrong or gets complicated during HC3 implementations? Where do clients get frustrated, and how does that reflect on their perception of what HC3 is capable of?"
  • Competitive Comparison
    "You see how clients talk about HC3 versus other vendors they've worked with. When clients compare HC3 to competitors, what do they consistently say we do better or worse? Give me specific examples."
  • Sales Support Gap
    "When the sales team is trying to position HC3's capabilities, what information or materials do they need that don't exist yet? What questions do prospects ask that are hard to answer with current materials?"
  • Client Success Stories
    "Tell me about a client where HC3 truly solved a problem that went beyond statements - where we actually delivered on 'operational efficiency.' What was the situation, and how did the client describe the value afterward?"

Track 3: Strategic Questions (Jeremiah James)

▼
  • Product-Market Reality
    "From a product perspective, where is HC3 actually ahead of the market, and where are we behind? What capabilities do we claim that aren't quite ready, and what capabilities do we have that we're underselling?"
  • Acquisition Integration
    "You came to HC3 through the Automail/DOC acquisition. What assumptions about the market or HC3's positioning turned out to be wrong once you got inside? What surprised you about how clients actually use HC3 versus how we think they use it?"
  • Strategic Blind Spots
    "If you had to identify the one strategic assumption that leadership is most attached to but might be wrong about, what would it be? Where do you see gaps between HC3's strategic vision and market reality?"
  • Technology Risk
    "With your security background, how do you see the AI/automation discussion playing out in financial services? Are FIs really backing away from AI solutions, or is that wishful thinking? What's the real technology anxiety you're hearing from clients?"
  • Competitive Threats
    "Looking at the roadmaps and market moves, who should HC3 be most worried about over the next 18 months? Not just the obvious competitors, but the companies that could redefine what 'customer communication management' means for FIs?"

Track 4: Business Development Questions (Heather Seiler)

▼
  • New Employee Reality Check
    "You joined HC3 9 months ago from outside the industry. What assumptions about HC3 or the market turned out to be wrong? How did prospects react to HC3 differently than you expected based on your onboarding?"
  • Prospect Conversation Breakdown
    "Walk me through a recent prospect call where you had to explain what HC3 does beyond statements. What language worked, what didn't land, and where did you see them lose interest or get confused?"
  • Competitive Positioning Reality
    "When prospects compare HC3 to competitors like Doxim or others, what do they consistently ask that's hard to answer? What competitive advantages do we claim that prospects don't seem to value as much as we think they should?"
  • Sales Material Gaps
    "What information do you wish you had in conversations that doesn't exist in current sales materials? When prospects ask 'How is HC3 different from [competitor]?' what would make that answer more compelling?"
  • Market Perception vs. Reality
    "Based on your BD conversations, how do FI prospects actually describe their communication challenges? Is it about statements, digital transformation, operational efficiency, or something else? How does that match or not match how HC3 positions itself?"
  • Ideal Positioning
    "If you could rewrite HC3's elevator pitch based on what you've learned in 9 months of prospect conversations, what would you emphasize? What would you de-emphasize or stop saying entirely?"

Track 5: Executive Leadership Questions (Tori VanCura-Rutland)

▼
  • Growth Strategy Reality Check
    "As Chief Growth Officer, you're driving HC3's expansion in a market where you're known primarily as a 'statement company.' Walk me through a recent conversation where you had to position HC3's broader capabilities. What resonated with prospects, and where did you see resistance?"
  • Competitive Positioning Challenges
    "When you're competing against companies like Doxim or Messagepoint, what's the one differentiator you wish prospects understood better? Give me a specific example of a deal where positioning made the difference - win or loss."
  • Market Evolution Opportunity
    "There's growing discussion about AI backlash creating opportunities for 'efficiency without risk' solutions. From your growth perspective, how real is this market shift, and how should HC3 capitalize on it without appearing opportunistic?"
  • Partnership and Channel Strategy
    "Looking at HC3's current partnership strategy and channel development, what's the biggest growth lever that's underutilized? Where do you see untapped market opportunities that better positioning could unlock?"
  • 18-Month Growth Vision
    "If HC3's positioning evolution is successful, how does your growth strategy change? What markets or customer segments become accessible that aren't today, and what would success look like in measurable terms?"
  • Internal Growth Enablement
    "From a growth execution standpoint, what messaging or positioning tools does your team need that don't exist today? What would make HC3 easier to sell and scale?"
