## Slide 1: Three Types of Acquisitions: Why We Chose the Operator's Path

**Opening:** "When we're thinking about acquisitions, there are really three strategic paths available. Let me walk you through why we landed where we did."

### 1. House of Brands Strategy
**When to use:** Strong brand recognition that deserves its own room
- **Example:** If we acquired a company with established industry presence—shows up in major analyst reports, industry events, well-known thought leadership—we'd create a house of brands. Like adding a fully decorated room to HC3, it slots right in with its own identity.
- **Why we didn't:** These acquisitions are extremely pricey. Few companies have that level of brand equity. Most available targets don't meet this threshold.

### 2. Platform Strategy
**When to use:** End-to-end technology you couldn't build in reasonable time
- **Example:** A complete core banking solution or comprehensive compliance suite that becomes "HC3 Platform." Customers buy the platform, then add modules like statements, payments, risk management.
- **Why we didn't:** Requires decoupling all solutions so they can be purchased as modules. Significant engineering work. More targets available, but still complex integration.

### 3. Micro Solutions / Sticky Note Problems
**When to use:** Highly specialized companies that fill operational gaps
- **Example:** Companies like Auto ABS—focused on one specific high-stakes operational challenge that FIs struggle with internally. Often surprisingly profitable with solid revenue despite narrow focus.
- **Why we chose this:** More acquisitions available, reasonable pricing, fits our operator's mindset. These usually build toward platform strategy over time—you buy the wedge, then expand the offering.

**Key Point:** "We're not locked into one approach forever. The typical progression is: Micro solutions → Platform strategy → House of brands. Each acquisition builds toward the next level as you gain cash, capabilities, and market position."

## Slide 2: The Acquisition-Ready Foundation

### 32-Second Elevator Pitch (Works for Any Acquisition)

**"HC3 is the operations partner for critical work community FIs can't afford to get wrong."**

We deliver measurable operational efficiency: significant annual cost savings per customer, substantial processing time reduction, and lower error rates. **400+ FIs** trust us because we handle the complex operational work their teams don't have the bandwidth or expertise to perfect.

### How Non-Statement Products Fit (Auto ABS Example)

**The Sticky Note Visual:**
- **Current reality:** We have 15 yellow sticky notes (all statement-related work) and 1 green sticky note (Auto ABS).
- **Customer perspective:** All 16 sticky notes represent the same thing—high-stakes operational work they don't have bandwidth to handle.
- **The insight:** Auto ABS fits our messaging strategy because it's adjacent work—specialized compliance and verification that requires the same operational discipline as statements.

**Key Point:** "From the customer's perspective, it's all the same category of work: critical operations they can't afford to get wrong."

### Competitive Advantage
This positioning creates market differentiation no competitor can match. Jack Henry and Fiserv sell core bundles. Doxim sells enterprise software. HC3 operates as the specialized partner for critical work that requires perfection—delivering measurable operational outcomes with room for strategic growth.

## Slide 3: Q&A

**Opening:** "I took a swing at what your questions probably are. Let's address these."

### Why now? Why not wait until we have a compelling business event?

**Answer:** The best time for strategic positioning is when you don't have pressure on your back. Like redecorating your house—you want to do it business-as-usual, not right before Christmas when family's coming. This gives us quality execution before any existential events, acquisitions, or staff changes force our hand.

### What's this going to cost us? What's the investment?

**Answer:** Positioning work is complete—that investment is behind us. Implementation is primarily internal effort with Kristen leading GTM activation. The ROI comes from improved digital conversion: we can increase our digital conversion from 2% to 2.5% within six months, based on recent client example (Nuvia achieved exactly this improvement using the same approach).

**If they ask for specifics on the math:** "We know our current conversion rate. Industry average for fintechs is about 2%. With better messaging, more conversion-focused website, and the go-to-market plan we have, we can lift that to 2.5%. Nuvia went from 2% to 2.5% doing exactly what we're doing, within six months."

### Bottom line: what should we hold you accountable for?

**Answer:** Three pipeline areas:
1. **Top of funnel:** Industry recognition that drives pipeline from events and analyst mentions—getting mentioned as an "operations partner" vs. "statements vendor"
2. **Mid funnel:** Digital channel conversion improvement from 2% to 2.5% within six months
3. **Post-sales:** Customer conversations shifting from price negotiations to expansion and cross-sell opportunities with existing logos
