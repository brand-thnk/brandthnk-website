Positioning Filters

Three Filter Tests

Click each filter to expand and see the specific criteria your copy should align with. Use these as reference while evaluating your draft copy above.

Filter 1: Is / Is Not
+
HC3 IS
An operational partner you trust with your most sensitive customer communications
HC3 IS NOT
A technology vendor selling features on a comparison chart
HC3 IS
A specialist in the work your core system was not built to do
HC3 IS NOT
An enterprise platform that looks identical in the demo and diverges in year two
HC3 IS
The team that takes ownership when something goes wrong and tells you before you have to ask
HC3 IS NOT
The vendor you manage through support tickets and SLA reports
Filter 2: Three Positioning Filters
+
Positioning Filter 1
HC3 is a family-owned company that runs inside your operations, not a platform trying to replace the core.
Positioning Filter 2
HC3 starts from where clients are today and adds capability without forcing a migration.
Positioning Filter 3
When HC3 runs your customer communications, executive teams stop discussing them at quarterly reviews.
Filter 3: Competitive Tier Check
+
vs. Core-Bundled (Jack Henry, Fiserv)
"Your core runs accounts. HC3 runs the communications those accounts depend on."
vs. Enterprise CCM (Doxim)
"The demo is identical. Ask what year two looks like."
vs. Peer Specialists (PrintMail, InfoImage)
"Everyone in this category runs statements. Ask what else they run."
