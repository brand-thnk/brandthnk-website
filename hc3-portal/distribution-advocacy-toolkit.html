Distribution Strategy Overview

Peer referral drives vendor discovery in banking. This toolkit prioritizes channels that maximize referral conversion while protecting HC3's limited bandwidth. Focus on depth over breadth.

Resource Reality Check

HC3's constraint: Small marketing team with operational demands. Strategy prioritizes high-impact, low-maintenance channels over comprehensive coverage. Better to execute 3-4 channels well than spread thin across 15.

Channel Priority Matrix

Protect at All Costs (Revenue Protection)

  • IBAT endorsement maintenance — Texas is HC3's strongest market. Maintain co-marketing materials and leverage existing relationship
  • Jack Henry ecosystem integration — Direct access to FI prospects. Strengthen integration and present at Connect events
  • Customer reference program — Facilitate peer calls for prospects. HC3's strongest conversion tool
  • Core conference presence — Financial Brand Forum, ICBA LIVE, CUNA GAC. Booth + networking minimum

Execute If Bandwidth Allows (High Impact, Manageable)

  • ICBA Endorsed Service Provider application — National community bank credibility. One-time effort, ongoing benefit
  • America's Credit Unions ESP application — Credit union market credibility. Mirrors ICBA approach
  • The Financial Brand contributed content — Monthly articles position HC3 as beyond-core specialist
  • Speaking opportunities — Target 2-3 speaking slots annually at core conferences

Defer or Scale Back (Nice-to-Have)

  • Secondary conference circuit — Money 20/20, BAI, etc. High cost, broad audience, lower FI density
  • Multiple trade publications — Focus on The Financial Brand first. Add ABA Banking Journal if capacity allows
  • Podcast appearances — Optional if clear capacity and guest-ready executives
  • State association expansion — Beyond initial 3-5 states, defer until platform launch proves successful

Conference Strategy

Avoid: Trade show tourism. HC3 attends to build relationships and generate leads, not collect swag. Every conference requires clear activation plan: booth goals, meeting targets, follow-up process.

Channel Activation Roadmap

Phase 1: Foundation (Immediate)

  • IBAT relationship maintenance — Quarterly check-ins, co-marketing material updates
  • Customer reference program setup — Identify 3-5 willing customers, create reference call process
  • Jack Henry integration strengthening — Technical integration improvements, relationship building

Phase 2: Credibility Building (Next 90 Days)

  • ICBA ESP application submission — Research requirements, complete application process
  • The Financial Brand content pipeline — Pitch 3 article topics, establish monthly cadence
  • Core conference speaking submissions — Submit for 2-3 speaking opportunities

Phase 3: Expansion (Capacity Permitting)

  • America's Credit Unions ESP application — Mirror ICBA approach for CU market
  • Secondary content channels — Add ABA Banking Journal, Credit Union Times
  • State association expansion — Target 2-3 additional state endorsements
