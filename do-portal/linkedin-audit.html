Five Core Problems

Inverted Content Mix
80% product announcements, 20% thought leadership. Should be reversed—humans engage with ideas and insights, not feature lists.
No Human Voice
Corporate posts lack individual perspective. Nate, Ted, and Adam's expertise is invisible. People connect with people, not companies.
Weak Visuals
Stock photos and generic graphics fail to capture attention. Successful fintech content uses data visualizations and authentic imagery.
Buried CTAs
No clear next steps for engaged readers. Comments like "Great insights!" have nowhere to go. Need conversation starters and lead capture.
Unclear Posting Logic
Random timing and frequency. No strategic relationship between post topics, audience behavior patterns, or business goals.

Strategic Action Framework

START
  • People-first content: Nate, Ted, Adam as voices with distinct perspectives
  • Data storytelling: Transform customer success metrics into compelling narratives
  • Industry commentary: DO's take on banking trends, not just news sharing
  • Customer spotlight series: Real bank stories with measurable outcomes
  • Behind-the-scenes content: Product development insights, team perspectives
STOP
  • Generic product announcements: Features without context or customer impact
  • Stock photo reliance: Fake imagery that signals corporate disconnect
  • One-way broadcasting: Posts that don't invite engagement or conversation
  • Industry jargon heavy copy: Language that excludes rather than includes
  • Random posting schedule: Content without strategic timing or audience consideration
CONTINUE
  • Consistent frequency: 2-3 posts weekly maintains presence
  • Industry report sharing: But add DO's perspective and commentary
  • Company milestone celebration: Build on successful founder story approach
  • Community banking focus: Audience alignment is strong, expand on this
  • Professional tone: Authoritative voice works, but add personality

Bottom Line

Digital Onboarding has the expertise, customer success stories, and market position to dominate LinkedIn thought leadership in digital banking engagement. The current approach—feature-heavy, voice-absent content—wastes this competitive advantage.

Immediate opportunity: Launch people-first LinkedIn strategy where three executives own thematic content with weekly personal posts amplified by the company page. Focus on data-driven digital banking engagement, lifecycle orchestration, and practical AI implementation.

90-day goal: Establish Nate, Ted, and Adam as the go-to voices for community banks seeking engagement strategy beyond account opening. Measure success through qualified lead generation, not vanity metrics.
