Monthly Update Template

Structured communication framework for analyst outreach

Use this template for monthly analyst updates, customizing content based on recipient focus areas and recent interactions:

Subject Line Formula

"Digital Onboarding Update: [Key Development/Topic] + Q&A Opportunity"

Examples: "Digital Onboarding Update: Channel Orchestration Insights + Q&A" or "Digital Onboarding Update: New Partnership Momentum + Executive Access"

Email Template Structure

Opening (Relationship Building):
"Hi [Name], hope you're well. Wanted to share some updates from Digital Onboarding that align with your recent research on [specific analyst focus area]."

Business Updates (2-3 bullet points):
• Partnership/integration updates (without specifics if embargoed)
• Customer traction signals or use case evolution
• Product development or capability expansion

Industry Insight (1 substantive point):
"From our client conversations, we're seeing [specific trend/pattern] around [relevant topic]. This aligns with your thesis on [analyst's recent content]."

Thought Leadership Signal:
Brief mention of executive content, speaking, or expertise areas (Nate's orchestration insights, data-driven engagement approaches)

Soft CTA:
"Happy to schedule a brief call if you'd like to dig into any of these trends or have questions about our approach to [relevant topic]."

Professional Close:
"Best regards,
[Sender Name]
[Title], Digital Onboarding
[Direct contact info]"

Monthly Execution Checklist

Systematic approach to analyst relationship management

Pre-Update Preparation

Review Analyst Recent Content
Check target analysts' recent reports, social posts, and commentary to identify connection points and relevant themes for personalization.
Compile Business Updates
Gather partnership news, customer traction signals, product developments, and executive content/speaking activities from the past month.
Identify Industry Insights
Extract 1-2 substantive trends or patterns from client conversations that provide unique market perspective to share with analysts.

Update Execution

Personalize Each Email
Customize the template for each analyst based on their focus areas, recent research, and previous interactions with Digital Onboarding.
Time Distribution Strategically
Send updates on Tuesday-Thursday, mid-morning to maximize open rates. Avoid Mondays, Fridays, and major industry event days.
Track Responses and Engagement
Log analyst responses, meeting requests, and follow-up opportunities in CRM for relationship building tracking and future reference.

Follow-Up Management

Schedule Requested Calls
Promptly schedule any analyst calls or briefings requested, involving appropriate DO executives based on discussion topics.
Provide Additional Resources
Share relevant case studies, executive interviews, or strategic documents when analysts express interest in deeper information.
Update Relationship Database
Record interactions, preferences, and future opportunity notes for each analyst to inform subsequent outreach and relationship building.
