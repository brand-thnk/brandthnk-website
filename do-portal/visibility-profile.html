1. Nate Shahan: Executive Bio

Nate Shahan is Chief Product Officer at Digital Onboarding, Inc., the leading digital engagement platform for banks and credit unions. The platform is trusted by 170+ financial institutions and built around a core thesis: growth isn't just about account opening, it's about what happens next.

Shahan brings more than two decades of experience building and scaling data-driven SaaS products for financial institutions, with deep expertise in product strategy and analytics. He has led the development of products that help banks and credit unions deepen customer relationships and increase revenues through more personalized experiences.

Most recently, Shahan was Co-founder and Chief Product Officer of Segmint, an analytics and engagement company that leveraged transaction data to deliver actionable customer insights to community financial institutions. He designed, developed, and patented data-driven products that help financial institutions turn transaction data into intelligence for cross-sell, acquisition, retention, and digital banking channel orchestration. Segmint was acquired by Alkami Technology for $135.5 million in 2022.

Following the acquisition, Shahan continued as a senior product executive at Alkami, helping integrate and expand analytics-driven capabilities across the platform. He presented at Alkami Co:Lab 2024 on the intersection of human intelligence and AI in financial services marketing.

Shahan holds a degree from The University of Akron and is based in Akron, Ohio.

3. Three Thought Leadership Topics for Nate Shahan

Each topic starts from a message DO already has in market and layers Nate's specific credibility on top. He is not creating new messaging. He is delivering DO's existing story with the authority of someone who has built the technology behind it. The structure for each topic is: DO's message (what the market needs to hear), Nate's passion point (why he's the right voice), and the compound effect (what happens when the two reinforce each other).

Topic 1: "It's Not About Account Opening. It's About What Happens Next."

DO Message

This is Digital Onboarding's core market thesis, straight from Nate's own CPO announcement: "Growth in financial services isn't just about account opening, it's about what happens next." The entire platform exists to solve the post-opening engagement gap. DO's solutions map to the lifecycle: onboarding, nurturing, cross-selling, servicing. Their proof points (375% growth in direct deposits, 64% reduction in attrition) are all about what happens after the account is opened.

Nate's Passion Point

The reason most FIs fail at "what happens next" is that they can't see their own customer data clearly enough to act on it. Nate spent 15+ years at Segmint building and patenting the technology that turns truncated, manipulated transaction data into actionable intelligence. He knows, from building it, that the gap between account opening and real engagement is a data problem disguised as a marketing problem.

The Compound

When Nate talks about lifecycle engagement, he's not repeating a marketing message. He's explaining why the data layer underneath DO's platform is what makes "what happens next" actually work through intelligent channel orchestration. No other CPO in the digital banking engagement space can make this case with the same technical proof. That's the differentiation.

Placement Targets

  • American Banker / PaymentsSource (byline: "The 90 Days After Digital Banking Onboarding That Determine Everything")
  • The Financial Brand (feature for digital banking leaders on the channel orchestration engagement gap)
  • BAI Banking Strategies (practitioner angle: what data do you need to close the digital banking lifecycle gap?)
  • Conference talk: American Banker Digital Banking 2026 (engagement or data track) - primary target

Topic 2: "Small Teams, Measurable Growth: The Engagement Math That Changes the Business Case"

DO Message

"We make small teams feel huge." This is on the DO homepage and it's one of the most powerful positioning lines they have. Paired with the proof points (40% growth in online debit card spending, 100% increase in credit card cross-sell rate, 27% reduction in phone calls), it reframes digital engagement from a marketing expense to a P&L driver. DO's automated targeting engine and ready-made campaign templates are built for resource-constrained teams at community FIs.

Nate's Passion Point

The shift from cost center to profit center is a product problem, not a marketing problem. At Alkami, Nate co-developed the thesis that successful digital transformation requires FIs to stop treating digital banking as a cost of doing business. His entire career has been about building products that deliver measurable outcomes from data, not just dashboards or reports. He knows what it takes to build a platform that actually moves the numbers for small teams.

The Compound

DO has the outcomes data. Nate has the product credibility to explain why those outcomes happen. When a community bank CMO hears that 170+ FIs are getting 375% growth in direct deposits, the next question is "how." Nate can answer that with product specificity that a marketing message alone cannot deliver.

Topic 3: "Before You Deploy AI, Connect Your Data"

DO Message

DO's Data Connector Tool imports data via API, SFTP, or CSV from core, digital banking, account opening, CRM, and third-party systems. Their automated targeting engine does the heavy lifting so teams don't need to create and upload lists. This is a data integration and activation story, not an AI story. But every FI is asking about AI right now, and DO's answer is: AI is only as useful as the data you feed it. For digital banking specifically, this means connecting transaction behavior to channel engagement patterns so institutions can orchestrate timely, relevant interactions across mobile, web, and in-person touchpoints. The data layer is the product.

Nate's Passion Point

He presented at Alkami Co:Lab 2024 on "Humans and AI: How to Maximize Marketing ROI." He didn't talk about AI as magic. He talked about the data infrastructure that makes AI useful. At Segmint, he built products that solved the foundational data problem: transaction data that arrives riddled with manipulation and truncation. He knows, from building it, that AI trained on fragmented, dirty data produces garbage outputs. His credibility on this topic is not theoretical. It's patented.

The Compound

Every competitor in the engagement space is rushing to add "AI-powered" to their messaging. DO can differentiate by going one layer deeper: the data infrastructure that makes AI actually work. Nate has built both the data layer (Segmint) and the engagement layer (now at DO). When he says "fix your data before you buy AI," it carries the weight of 20 years of building the products that prove it.

4. Three Ideas to Productize Nate Shahan

Productizing a person means turning their expertise into a repeatable, visible asset that generates inbound attention, customer trust, and content simultaneously. These ideas go beyond "write a blog post." They create formats that compound.

Idea 1: "The Data Layer" — A 3-Episode Video Series with DO Customers

Format

Short-form video series (15-20 min per episode). Nate interviews a DO customer or friendly about a specific data and engagement challenge they solved. Produced for LinkedIn, YouTube, and the DO website.

Why This Works

  • Puts Nate in the host seat, which positions him as the expert leading the conversation, not pitching
  • Generates customer proof points without asking for a formal case study (lower friction than a written case study, higher trust than a testimonial quote)
  • Creates 3 episodes of reusable content: full video, clips for social, pull quotes, LinkedIn posts, blog recaps, newsletter fodder
  • Gives DO an owned content property with a brand name that can scale beyond 3 episodes

Idea 2: "Nate's Notebook" — A Monthly LinkedIn Data Insight Series

Format

One LinkedIn post per month from Nate's personal account that takes a single data point, trend, or customer insight and unpacks what it means for FI strategy. Short, opinionated, specific. Built to provoke comment and sharing.

Sample Post Angles

  1. "We're working with 170+ FIs. The ones winning at engagement aren't the ones with the best app. They're the ones with the cleanest data."
  2. "Every FI I talk to wants AI-powered personalization. Almost none of them can tell me what data they're feeding it."
  3. "The average community FI has 5 systems that know different things about the same customer. That's not a technology problem. It's a product problem."
  4. "Attrition doesn't start when someone leaves. It starts 90 days after they open the account and nobody reaches out." (ties to DO's 64% attrition reduction stat)

Idea 3: "The CPO Circuit" — 5 Podcast Placements in 90 Days

Format

A targeted podcast placement campaign. Identify 5 podcasts where Nate's expertise on data, AI, and lifecycle engagement in banking is a direct fit. Pitch him with pre-packaged talking points and episode concepts tailored to each host.

Target Podcasts

  1. Banking Transformed with Jim Marous (The Financial Brand) — largest audience in digital banking
  2. Fintech One-on-One with Peter Renton — deep dives on fintech product strategy
  3. Breaking Banks with Brett King — global audience, data and innovation focus
  4. CUInsight — strong CU audience, Nate's track record serving community FIs is a direct fit
  5. BAI Banking Strategies Podcast — practitioner audience, data and analytics themes

5. The Operating Model: Nate as DO's Data Voice

Productizing a person works like a product release. It needs a launch and a cadence. The goal is not a one-time PR push. It is to make Nate the face and voice of every new market theme or message DO puts out around data. When Nate's visibility grows, DO's visibility grows. When DO ships a data story, Nate delivers it.

The Principle Nate is the delivery vehicle for DO's data story. Every new capability, customer proof point, or market insight should route through him as the voice. This creates a single, recognizable point of authority that analysts, editors, and event organizers learn to associate with DO and data-driven engagement.

6. Content Automation: AirOps

As Nate's content cadence ramps up (monthly LinkedIn posts, podcast appearances, bylines, analyst updates), the repurposing workload grows fast. AirOps (airops.com) offers a free tier that can automate the repetitive parts of content production without adding headcount.

What AirOps Can Do for This Program

  • Auto-generate social media variants from a single Notebook post (LinkedIn, X, short-form for DO company page)
  • Repurpose podcast transcripts into blog draft summaries, pull quote cards, and newsletter snippets
  • Create first-draft analyst update emails from a structured input template (Nate or DO team fills in bullet points, AirOps formats and drafts the narrative)
  • Generate SEO-optimized meta descriptions and headers for bylines published on DO's blog
Why This Matters: The biggest risk to a productization program is not quality. It is consistency. Automation removes the friction that causes cadence to slip. When the monthly Notebook post can spin into 4 social posts, an analyst update paragraph, and a blog excerpt in 15 minutes instead of 2 hours, the program stays on track.
