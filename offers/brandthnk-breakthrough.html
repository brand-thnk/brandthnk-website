BrandThnk Breakthrough

An eight-week engagement for fintech brands that are generating activity but not breaking through

You have a real product. The market doesn't know it yet.

You've invested in marketing. The content is going out, the campaigns are running, and the pipeline still isn't moving the way it should. The instinct is to do more -- more content, more channels, more spend.

The problem is rarely volume. It's almost always clarity. When your market can't quickly understand what you do, why it matters, and why you're the right choice, more activity makes the noise louder without making the signal stronger.

What it costs to stay here.

Market windows in fintech close fast. The position you should own doesn't stay open while you figure out your messaging. Competitors with weaker products take it because they got clear first.

The harder problem is internal. When your team can't agree on what the brand stands for, every campaign starts from scratch and every new hire learns a different version of the story. That's not a marketing problem. It's a drag on the whole business.

What the engagement produces.

The Breakthrough is eight weeks of focused work to find what's creating noise, build the brand platform that replaces it, and stay in it with you until the new positioning is in market and pulling.

The work moves in four phases:

Discovery (Weeks 1-2)

Stakeholder interviews, a fast audit of existing brand and messaging, and a Strategy Brief that gives your team a shared lens before any new work begins.

Positioning (Week 3)

A messaging sprint that produces a Brand Positioning Map and Messaging Framework, from vision level down to campaign-ready language.

Go-to-Market (Week 4)

A 90-Day GTM Activation Plan built around your business reality, with milestones, channel priorities, and content hooks -- each with an owner.

Execution (Weeks 5-8)

Distribution planning, internal team prep, KPI setup, and a final Executive Strategy Session with your C-suite or GTM leaders to align leadership and map next-phase priorities.

Who runs this work.

Allison Netzer has spent 25 years at the intersection of brand strategy and revenue, as a CMO, an enterprise sales leader, and an operator who carried a bag. She has run this engagement with over 50 fintech and financial institution brands.

The Breakthrough is grounded in that experience, not in a generic consulting playbook, and it starts with this question: If you stripped your logo from everything you published in the last 90 days, would anyone know it was you?

If the answer is no, that's not a content problem. It's a brand problem, and it doesn't fix itself.
