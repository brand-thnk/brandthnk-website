MOTION 1: Events + Compelling Collateral

Why This Is First

Your team is already focused here. Events create the fastest path to qualified conversations and board-level introductions. Collateral can be refreshed in 2-3 weeks. ROI is highest relative to effort.

MOTION 3: Industry Advisory Board (IAB)

Why This Is Third

Creates instant credibility with peer-operated governing body. Repeatable relationship engine that generates 2-3 warm introductions per member per quarter.

What's Already Ready

  • Full IAB design (8-12 members, profile mix, selection criteria)
  • Recruitment templates and outreach language
  • Meeting format and cadence (45 min per quarter + optional 1:1s)
  • Value exchange framework (what members get vs. what you get)

What Still Needs Development

  • Member shortlist finalization (targeting former CU CEOs, CROs, digital officers, league leaders)
  • Outreach execution and meeting scheduling
  • Compensation model decision (paid annual honorarium vs. value-exchange model)
  • Q2 IAB agenda and pre-work material

Business Impact

16-36
Warm intros per year (8 members @ 2-3 intros/quarter)
30-40%
Warm intro conversion rate (vs 5-10% cold)

  • Research partnership with Datos can be positioned as "IAB insight validation"

Resource Requirement

Medium. Jason owns member recruitment. Theresa handles meeting facilitation and follow-up. Allison provides ongoing advisory support.

MOTION 4: Datos Insights Research Partnership

Why This Is Fourth

Borrowed third-party credibility. "Proof beyond our word." Research asset becomes permanent sales tool and shared conference stage opportunity.

What's Already Ready

  • Research angles defined (delinquency trends, payment behavior, ROI of modernization)
  • Datos contact relationship established
  • Scope outline and timeline

What Still Needs Development

  • Theresa to brief Datos on LoanPay angles and key insights you want validated
  • Scope confirmation and deliverable format (report, infographic, webinar, case studies)
  • Cut-down assets for sales use (2-3 clean takeaways)

Business Impact

  • Research validates core claims around delinquency, payment behavior, staffing pressure
  • Permanent asset for sales collateral
  • Potential co-branded promotion (Datos + CheckAlt)
  • Conference speaking opportunity or panel participation

Resource Requirement

Low. Theresa owns the briefing. Datos owns research development. Marketing handles promotion and asset cut-down.

Timeline

Week 1: Theresa brief to Datos.
Week 2-3: Scope confirmation.
Week 4-8: Datos research development (internal timeline).

MOTION 5: Executive Workshop (90 minutes)

Why This Is Fifth

Aligns leadership team around positioning, activation, and metrics. Prerequisite for scaling any of the above motions. Can be scheduled immediately.

What's Already Ready

  • Full facilitator guide with modules on market context, positioning, activation strategy, metrics and success measures
  • Participant roles and pre-work assignments
  • Complete talk tracks and discussion prompts for alignment
  • Example success metrics and quarterly check-ins

What Still Needs Development

  • Calendar a date with Jason and leadership team (recommend within 2-3 weeks)
  • Pre-work material distributed and completed by participants (2 weeks prior to workshop)
  • Post-workshop success metrics dashboard and tracking cadence

Business Impact

  • Leadership alignment on messaging and positioning (eliminates mixed signals in market)
  • Clear ownership assignment on Rolling Thunder phases
  • Shared metrics and success criteria (aligns quarterly reviews with activation progress)

Resource Requirement

Low. Scheduling only. Allison facilitates (90 min). Post-workshop follow-up owned by Jason/Theresa.

Timeline

Immediate: Schedule within 2-3 weeks. Pre-work distributed 2 weeks prior. Workshop: 90 minutes. Post-workshop: quarterly check-in cadence.

What to Do If Resources Are Limited — Strategic Triage

If you have bandwidth for all 5 motions: Execute in order. Stagger by 1-2 weeks. Parallel is better than sequential.

If resources are limited, priority sequence is:

  • Events + Collateral (highest ROI per effort)
  • CUSO Channel (highest multiplier effect)
  • Executive Workshop (prerequisite for alignment)
  • IAB (relationship engine, takes longer to build)
  • Datos (permanent asset but lower immediate urgency)
