Five Frameworks to Operationalize Brand Thinking

01
F/M/B Mapping

Clarify who owns brand execution across your organization without rigid silos.

Use when: You're unclear on who owns brand execution
02
Box→Edge Positioning

Establish category clarity before differentiation. Box first, edge second.

Use when: Customers can't quickly explain what you do
03
Pain→Promise→Proof

Build narrative that matches how brains make decisions. Feel before think.

Use when: You're leading with features instead of outcomes
04
Assumption Audit

Surface beliefs that may be limiting growth. Question what "everyone knows."

Use when: You suspect your strategy is built on outdated beliefs
05
Creative Brief

Set the standard before work begins. Your brand's salt shaker.

Use when: Projects keep drifting off-message

How to Get the Most From These Frameworks

  • Do them with your team. Misalignment surfaces faster in groups.
  • Write rough first drafts. Perfection kills momentum.
  • Test outputs with real customers or prospects. Internal consensus isn't validation.
  • Revisit quarterly. Markets shift. Assumptions expire.

Recommended Order

These templates work independently, but they build on each other. If you're starting from scratch:

  1. Assumption Audit — Surface what you think you know
  2. Box→Edge Positioning — Establish clarity before differentiation
  3. Pain→Promise→Proof — Build your narrative system
  4. F/M/B Mapping — Clarify who owns execution
  5. Creative Brief — Set standards for every project
