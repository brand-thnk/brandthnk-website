# M&A Persona Interview Protocol
## C-Suite Stakeholder Brand Evaluation for Fintech Acquisitions

**Service:** Fintech Brand Due Diligence
**Purpose:** Evaluate target company brand strength through key stakeholder perspectives
**Based on:** BrandThnk 3-Phase Interview Methodology (57-Question Library)
**Adapted for:** M&A acquisition evaluation context

---

## Protocol Overview

**Methodology:** 3-Phase interview simulation with 5 C-suite personas
**Duration:** 11-turn interview per persona (5 personas = 55 total interactions)
**Objective:** Assess how different stakeholders perceive target company's brand strength and integration risks

### Three-Phase Structure
- **Phase 1:** Context Setting (2-3 turns) - Acquisition scenario and persona baseline
- **Phase 2:** Brand Strength Analysis (4-8 turns) - Target evaluation across brand dimensions
- **Phase 3:** M&A Impact Synthesis (1-2 turns) - Integration assessment and recommendations

---

## Persona 1: CEO Patricia McKinney
### Strategic Decision Maker - Acquisition Authority

**M&A Focus:** Strategic positioning, competitive advantage, board-level confidence
**Evaluation Lens:** "How does this acquisition enhance our competitive position and enterprise value?"

#### Phase 1: Context Setting (2-3 turns)

**Turn 1: Acquisition Context**
*You're considering acquiring [TARGET COMPANY], a fintech company in the [CATEGORY] space. As CEO, you need to evaluate whether their brand strength justifies the acquisition premium and enhances your strategic positioning. Given your experience leading successful technology investments, what's your initial framework for evaluating brand value in M&A?*

**Turn 2: Strategic Baseline**
*Your board expects clear rationale for any acquisition over $10M. They've seen fintech deals where strong technology didn't translate to customer retention post-close. How important is the target company's brand reputation vs. their technology capabilities in your decision framework?*

**Turn 3: Competitive Context**
*[TARGET COMPANY] competes in a crowded fintech market with players like [COMPETITOR 1] and [COMPETITOR 2]. From a strategic perspective, how do you evaluate whether their brand differentiation is defensible post-acquisition, or if it's just marketing language?*

#### Phase 2: Brand Strength Analysis (4-6 turns)

**Turn 4: Market Recognition**
*Based on what you know about [TARGET COMPANY], how would you assess their market recognition and reputation within the fintech community? What evidence would convince you they have genuine brand awareness vs. just good PR?*

**Turn 5: Competitive Positioning**
*How do you perceive [TARGET COMPANY]'s competitive positioning? Are they a category leader, strong challenger, or follower? What would it mean for your own competitive position to acquire them?*

**Turn 6: Pricing Power Assessment**
*One test of brand strength is pricing power - the ability to charge premium prices without losing customers. What evidence would you look for to determine if [TARGET COMPANY] has true pricing power vs. being commoditized?*

**Turn 7: Customer Loyalty Evidence**
*Customer retention during acquisition is critical for deal success. How would you evaluate [TARGET COMPANY]'s customer loyalty and the likelihood they'll retain customers through an acquisition process?*

**Turn 8: Strategic Value**
*Beyond their current customer base, how would you assess whether [TARGET COMPANY]'s brand helps or hurts your ability to win new business and compete with larger fintech platforms?*

**Conditional Turn 9: Integration Concerns**
*If persona expresses skepticism: What specific brand-related integration risks concern you most, and how would you mitigate them?*

#### Phase 3: M&A Impact Synthesis (1-2 turns)

**Turn 9/10: Enterprise Value Impact**
*Considering everything we've discussed, how would you quantify [TARGET COMPANY]'s brand contribution to enterprise value? Would their brand justify paying a premium, or should it be discounted due to integration risks?*

**Turn 10/11: Board Recommendation**
*You need to present this acquisition to your board. What would be your 2-3 key points about [TARGET COMPANY]'s brand strength and how it affects the strategic rationale for this deal?*

## Persona 2: CDO Marcus Chen
### Digital Transformation Champion - Customer Experience Focus

**M&A Focus:** Customer experience differentiation, digital brand perception, retention risk
**Evaluation Lens:** "How does this brand enhance our digital transformation story and customer experience?"

#### Phase 1: Context Setting (2-3 turns)

**Turn 1: Digital Brand Context**
*As CDO, you're evaluating [TARGET COMPANY] for acquisition to enhance your digital capabilities. You know that fintech acquisitions often struggle with customer experience integration. How do you evaluate a target company's brand strength from a digital transformation perspective?*

**Turn 2: Customer Experience Baseline**
*Your customers expect seamless digital experiences. They're skeptical of acquisitions that might disrupt service quality. How important is the target's digital brand reputation vs. their technology platform capabilities?*

**Turn 3: Competitive Digital Positioning**
*The fintech space moves quickly, and customer expectations are shaped by leaders like Stripe, Plaid, and Square. How do you assess whether [TARGET COMPANY] is perceived as a digital leader or follower by their customers?*

#### Phase 2: Brand Strength Analysis (5-7 turns)

**Turn 4: Digital Brand Perception**
*How do you perceive [TARGET COMPANY]'s digital brand and customer experience reputation? What signals tell you whether they're viewed as innovative vs. traditional by their users?*

**Turn 5: Customer Experience Evidence**
*What evidence would convince you that [TARGET COMPANY]'s customers have positive experiences that would survive an acquisition integration? How do you evaluate customer experience strength?*

**Turn 6: Digital Community Standing**
*How does [TARGET COMPANY] stand with developer communities, early adopters, and digital-first customers? Do they have credibility with the audiences you're trying to reach?*

**Turn 7: Brand-Customer Alignment**
*Does [TARGET COMPANY]'s brand promise align with what customers actually experience? How do you assess the gap between brand messaging and customer reality?*

**Turn 8: Integration Impact on CX**
*How would acquiring [TARGET COMPANY] affect your ability to deliver consistent customer experiences? Would their brand help or complicate your digital transformation messaging?*

**Conditional Turn 9: Digital Differentiation**
*If positive response: What specific digital brand advantages does [TARGET COMPANY] bring that would enhance your competitive position?*

#### Phase 3: M&A Impact Synthesis (1-2 turns)

**Turn 9/10: Customer Retention Risk**
*What's your assessment of customer retention risk if you acquire [TARGET COMPANY]? How does their brand strength affect the likelihood of keeping customers through integration?*

**Turn 10/11: Digital Strategy Fit**
*How would [TARGET COMPANY]'s brand fit into your overall digital transformation strategy? Would this acquisition accelerate or complicate your digital brand positioning?*

**M&A Focus:** Technical reputation, security standing, operational resilience

**Turn 1: Technical Due Diligence Context**
*As CIO, you're responsible for evaluating [TARGET COMPANY]'s technical capabilities and reputation. In fintech M&A, technical debt and security issues often surface post-close. How do you evaluate a target's technical brand reputation during due diligence?*

**Turn 2: Security and Compliance Reputation**
*Financial services demand the highest security and compliance standards. Your customers and regulators expect enterprise-grade security. How important is the target's security reputation vs. their current compliance certifications?*

**Turn 3: Technical Community Standing**
*Developer trust and technical community perception often predict integration success. How do you assess whether [TARGET COMPANY] has credibility with technical stakeholders who would be involved in integration?*

#### Phase 2: Brand Strength Analysis (5-7 turns)

**Turn 4: Technical Reputation Assessment**
*What's your assessment of [TARGET COMPANY]'s technical reputation in the fintech community? How do you evaluate whether they're perceived as technically competent vs. just good at marketing?*

**Turn 5: Security Brand Evaluation**
*How do you perceive [TARGET COMPANY]'s security and compliance reputation? What evidence tells you whether they have genuine security discipline vs. just certifications?*

**Turn 6: Operational Resilience**
*Fintech customers expect 99.9%+ uptime and seamless service. How would you evaluate [TARGET COMPANY]'s operational resilience and their brand reputation for reliability?*

**Turn 7: Integration Risk Assessment**
*From a technical integration perspective, how does [TARGET COMPANY]'s brand reputation affect the risk profile? Do they have a track record of successful technical partnerships?*

**Turn 8: Vendor Ecosystem Standing**
*How is [TARGET COMPANY] perceived by other technology vendors and integration partners? Strong ecosystem relationships often predict integration success.*

**Conditional Turn 9: Technical Concerns**
*If concerns expressed: What specific technical brand risks would you need to mitigate before recommending this acquisition?*

#### Phase 3: M&A Impact Synthesis (1-2 turns)

**Turn 9/10: Technical Integration Risk**
*Considering [TARGET COMPANY]'s technical brand reputation, what's your assessment of integration risk and complexity? How does their reputation affect your confidence in successful technical integration?*

**Turn 10/11: Operational Impact**
*How would acquiring [TARGET COMPANY] affect our overall operational risk profile and technical brand reputation in the market?*

## Cross-Persona Analysis Framework

### Pattern Identification
After completing all 5 persona interviews, analyze patterns across responses:

1. **Consensus Areas:** Where all personas agree on brand strength/weakness
2. **Divergent Views:** Where personas disagree on brand perception
3. **Risk Concentration:** Which personas identify similar integration risks
4. **Value Alignment:** Which personas see clear acquisition value
5. **Implementation Priorities:** What each persona needs to see for success

### Strategic Synthesis Questions
1. **Overall Brand Strength:** Based on all persona perspectives, is this a brand asset or liability?
2. **Integration Complexity:** Which stakeholder concerns present the highest integration risk?
3. **Value Creation:** Where do personas see the clearest paths to value creation?
4. **Success Factors:** What would need to be true for each persona to view this acquisition as successful?
5. **Deal Recommendations:** Should you proceed, what are the top 3 integration priorities?

## Quality Control Standards

### Interview Consistency
- Each persona maintains character throughout 11-turn interview
- Responses reflect established persona motivations and decision frameworks
- Follow-up questions adapt based on previous responses
- Conditional logic triggers appropriate for persona type

### Evidence Requirements
- Personas cite specific examples when making assessments
- Claims about brand strength backed by observable evidence
- Competitive comparisons grounded in market reality
- Risk assessments include specific mitigation considerations

### Analysis Reliability
- Cross-persona patterns validated against external data
- Stakeholder assessments benchmarked against industry standards
- Integration risk factors quantified where possible
- Recommendations tied to specific persona insights
