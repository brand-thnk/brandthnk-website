Brand Diagnostic

Find out what's actually working.

This is the same diagnostic we run at the start of every BrandThnk engagement. Three phases, each building on the last. Five minutes of honest answers tells you whether your brand is creating pull or just creating activity.

Phase 1 of 3

Is your brand creating pull?

Pull converts. Activity looks like progress but costs you time and money without moving the number that matters. Answer five yes-or-no questions honestly.

Question 1 of 5
If you removed your logo from everything you published in the last 90 days, would anyone know it was you?
This tests distinctiveness. If your content and collateral are interchangeable with a competitor's, your brand is not doing its job.
Can your sales team explain what makes you different without a deck or a script?
If the brand lives in a PDF but not in the hallway, your message is stuck in the slide deck. The real test is the unscripted explanation.
Do prospects compare you to the right competitors?
When your market puts you in the wrong category, they cannot evaluate your difference. Category confusion kills conversion before the conversation starts.
Does your marketing generate inbound conversations, not just impressions?
Impressions measure reach. Conversations measure pull. If you are getting views but not inbound interest, the brand is not working.
If five employees independently described what your company does, would you get the same answer?
Internal alignment is the first signal. If your leadership team cannot agree on what the company does, your salespeople will tell different stories.

Phase 3 of 3

Write your position.

This is the Box-Edge framework. It forces clarity on two things every brand needs: what category you own and what makes you better in that category. If you cannot fill both formulas, that is your diagnosis.

The Box
What category do you own?
"We are a [category] for [specific segment]."

If your market cannot place you in a category, they cannot evaluate your difference. Start with the Box.

0 words
The Edge
What is your actual difference?
"Unlike [status quo], we [mechanism] so you can [outcome]."

The Edge is not a feature list. It is the single thing you do differently that produces a result your market cares about. One sentence. No hedging.
