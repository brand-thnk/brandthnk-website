Tool 01 of 03

The Signal Test

Your brand is either generating signal or noise. Signal creates pull. Noise creates activity that looks like progress but doesn't convert. Five questions. Honest answers only.

If you removed your logo from everything you published in the last 90 days, would anyone know it was you?
This tests distinctiveness. If your content, ads, and collateral are interchangeable with a competitor's, your brand isn't doing its job.
Can your sales team explain what makes you different without reading from a deck or a script?
If the brand lives in a PDF but not in the conversation, you have a message that doesn't travel. The real test is the hallway explanation.
Do prospects compare you to the right competitors?
If your market puts you in the wrong category or compares you to companies you don't consider competitors, your Box is broken. They can't evaluate your Edge until the Box is clear.
Does your marketing generate inbound conversations, not just impressions?
Impressions are activity. Conversations are signal. If your marketing is generating views but not qualified interest, the brand isn't converting attention to pull.
If five employees independently described what your company does, would you get the same answer?
Internal alignment is the canary. If the leadership team can't agree on what the company stands for, no amount of external messaging will land consistently.

The One-Breath Test

Can you explain your brand difference in one breath?

If it takes more than 25 words, your message needs work. If you can't fill the sentence, your positioning needs work.

Tool 03 of 03

Box-Edge Workshop

If they can't box you, they won't buy you. Your brain is a pattern-recognition machine. When a prospect encounters your brand, it asks one question first: what drawer do you belong in? If the answer doesn't come fast, they move on. Define your category (Box), then sharpen your difference (Edge).

Part 1
The Box: Category Clarity
We're a [category] for [segment].

Use words real people use. Not internal shorthand. Not invented categories. The words your buyer would say.

Can a stranger categorize you in five seconds? That's your Box.
Part 2
The Edge: Differentiation
Unlike [status quo], we [mechanism] so you can [outcome], [proof].

The Edge assumes the Box is already working. If you're writing an Edge before the Box is clear, you're sharpening the wrong thing.

Once categorized, can a prospect articulate your difference in ten seconds?

The Two-Sentence Test

Say your Box and Edge together, out loud. If it takes more than one breath, simplify. If you need to explain it before it lands, the work isn't done.
